# Examples

Drop 5-10 of your best past emails in this folder. One per file. Name them with dates:

```
2025-01-15-promo-cohort-launch.md
2025-02-03-nurture-story-customer-turnaround.md
2025-02-20-announcement-podcast-launch.md
```

## What the system does with these

The voice files (`context/brand-voice.md` etc.) describe your voice in the abstract. Your examples *demonstrate* it. Claude reads both — the abstract rules AND the concrete examples — when drafting.

Examples catch what the voice files miss. Rhythm. Transitions. The specific move you pull when you hit a certain kind of paragraph. Your actual vocabulary in context.

## Which emails to include

Pick emails that:

- **Performed well.** Open rate, click rate, reply volume, whatever you measure. Your best emails teach the system what "good" looks like.
- **Sound the most like you.** Not the most polished — the most *you*. If an email came out sounding corporate even though it performed, it's not a good example.
- **Cover different formats.** At least one promo, one nurture, one announcement. More coverage = better drafts across the board.
- **Are recent.** Last 12 months is ideal. Voice drifts over time.

## Format each file with a light header

Helps Claude parse them and helps you remember the context later.

```
# 2025-01-15: Cohort launch promo

**Format:** promo
**Audience segment:** primary
**Result:** 42% open, 8.3% CTR, 127 enrollments
**Subject:** The 12-week system that pulled Jessica out of the money spiral

---

[paste the full email body here, including PS and signature]
```

The header isn't required — pasting the email alone works. The header just makes it easier later.
