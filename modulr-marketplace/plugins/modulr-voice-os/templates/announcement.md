# Announcement Template

A news, launch, or update email. Something has happened or is happening — the email's job is to inform, generate interest, and (optionally) direct action.

---

## When to use this template

- Product launch or relaunch
- New content release (podcast episode, YouTube video, blog post, guide)
- Event invitation (webinar, live workshop, in-person event)
- Major company news (partnership, milestone, new hire, rebrand)
- Update to an existing offer (new features, new pricing, new cohort dates)

**Don't use this template for:**
- Pure promotional pushes (use `promo.md` — the angle and proof structure there is sharper)
- Story-driven teaching content (use `nurture.md`)

---

## The core rule of announcements

**Lead with what's new, not with setup.** The reader opened the email to find out what happened. Tell them.

The fastest mistake: three paragraphs of context before the actual news.

---

## Required inputs

- **The news:** the one-sentence summary of what's new
- **Why it matters:** what's different for the reader now
- **The specific ask:** what you want them to do (read, watch, sign up, share, wait)
- **Proof or detail:** stats, quotes, specifics that make it real (sourced from `context/offers.md` or `briefs/`)
- **Timing:** when (if relevant — launch date, event date, deadline)

---

## Structure

### Subject line
Announcement subject lines can lean on news/novelty more than promo or nurture. Patterns that work:

- Direct: "`{{Thing}}` is live"
- Specific: "`{{Thing}}` — `{{key detail}}`"
- Invitation: "You're invited: `{{event}}`"
- Benefit-forward: "`{{What you'll get}}`"

Still run the hook framework — specific, clear, no clickbait.

### Preview text
Often the best use of preview text in announcements: the time/date detail or the one-line benefit.

### Opening (1-2 short paragraphs)
*Move:* state the news. No warm-up.

Good openers:
- "`{{Thing}}` launches `{{date}}`."
- "We just released `{{thing}}`."
- "I want to invite you to `{{event}}`."
- "Big news: `{{headline}}`."

Avoid:
- "I've been working on something for months and I'm finally ready to share it..."
- "For a while now, I've been thinking about..."
- Any framing that delays the news by more than two sentences

### The detail (1-3 paragraphs)
*Move:* explain what this is and why it's worth the reader's attention.

Cover:
- What it actually is (one sentence)
- Who it's for (one sentence — filter in / filter out)
- What's included or what will happen (specifics)
- The "why now" (if relevant)

Use bullets when listing specifics — bullets work well in announcements because readers scan.

### Proof or credibility (optional, 1 paragraph)
*Move:* if there's a stat, testimonial, or credibility marker that makes the news land harder, include it. Must be sourced in `context/offers.md`.

Common uses:
- "`{{X}}` people have already `{{Y}}`"
- A quote from a previous attendee / customer / collaborator
- A track record reference ("our last `{{thing}}` did `{{result}}`")

### The ask (1 short paragraph)
*Move:* one specific action. Name it.

Possibilities:
- "Read it here: [link]"
- "Save your seat: [link]"
- "Reply with `{{answer to question}}`"
- "Watch now: [link]"

### Close (1-2 short paragraphs OR a PS)
*Move:* reinforce one detail. The deadline. The bonus. The filter ("this is for people who...").

### PS (optional)
Announcements are one of the best places for a PS. Use it for:
- The deadline restated
- An answer to the objection you know the reader is having
- One more proof point

## Length

- **Short announcement:** 100-250 words (most event invites, content releases)
- **Standard announcement:** 250-500 words (product updates, launches)
- **Launch announcement:** 500-900 words (new offer, major milestone)

Don't pad announcements. If the news fits in 150 words, ship 150 words.

---

## Rhythm and voice check

- Did the email state the news in the first two sentences? If not, rewrite the open.
- Is the ask clear and singular? If the email has 3 CTAs, it has none.
- Run against `context/anti-ai-phrases.md`
- Em-dash count ≤ 1
- Confirm every claim and number is sourced

---

## Common announcement anti-patterns

- **Burying the lede:** three paragraphs of context before naming what's new
- **Multiple asks:** "read this, and also sign up for this, and also share this" — pick one
- **Over-hyping:** "biggest launch ever" / "most exciting news" — let the specifics carry it
- **Vague timing:** "coming soon" without a date — give a real window or don't mention timing
