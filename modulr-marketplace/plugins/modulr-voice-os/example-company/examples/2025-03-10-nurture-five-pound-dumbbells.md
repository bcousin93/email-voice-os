# 2025-03-10 Nurture: Five-Pound Dumbbells

**Format:** nurture
**Audience segment:** primary (both Rebuilder and First-Timer)
**Result:** 38% open rate, 6.1% CTR, 47 replies (highest reply count of the quarter)
**Subject:** Five-pound dumbbells are not doing what you think they are
**Preview:** And the reason isn't what most people think

---

Last Thursday, Karen emailed me a photo of her at the gym, standing next to a rack of dumbbells, holding two fives.

"I've been consistent for six months," she wrote. "Why do I still feel exactly the same?"

Karen's not doing anything wrong. She's doing what every fitness class in the last 20 years has told her to do: light weights, high reps, three sets of 15.

The reason it isn't working is that the stimulus is too small.

Your muscles don't build because you move them. They build because you challenge them past what they can currently do. If five pounds is easy at rep one and still easy at rep fifteen, your muscles have no reason to get stronger. They already did that work yesterday. And the day before. And two years ago.

The research on this is not ambiguous. The minimum effective load for muscle growth in women over 40 is roughly 70% of your one-rep max. For a new lifter, that's usually more weight than she's ever picked up. For Karen, it turned out to be a 25-pound dumbbell. Five times heavier than what she'd been using all year.

Here's what happened when Karen switched. Week one with the heavier weight, she could barely get through two sets. Week four, she was doing three sets at the same weight without struggle. Week eight, we bumped her to 30 pounds. Six months in, she's overhead pressing 40-pound dumbbells for sets of 8.

She did not get "bulky." She got strong. Her posture changed. Her back pain (which had been constant for a decade) stopped. She can carry both of her kids' backpacks and a grocery bag without putting anything down.

The five-pound dumbbells weren't the problem. The story about the five-pound dumbbells was the problem.

If you've been consistent for months or years and feel the same, it's probably not your willpower. It's probably the weight.
