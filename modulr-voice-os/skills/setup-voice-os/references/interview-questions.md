# Interview question bank

Pre-written multiple-choice stems for the wizard. Use these to keep the interview tight and non-technical.

## Step 0 — examples gate

**Q: Do you have 5+ of your best past emails ready to drop in?**
- Yes, I have them ready
- I can pull together 3-5 right now
- I have fewer than 3 — what do I do?

## Step 1 — brand voice

**Q: Who is the voice behind these emails?**
- A single named person (founder, CEO, principal)
- A character or persona
- A brand collective ("we")
- Mix — sometimes "I," sometimes "we"

**Q: From your examples, your average sentence length looks like [observation]. Which best describes how you want it to land?**
- Mostly short and punchy
- Mostly medium, occasional long
- Heavily mixed — short and long deliberately
- Mostly long and conversational

**Q: Contractions in your writing?**
- Always — I write how I talk
- Mostly, but not in headlines
- Sometimes
- Rarely or never

**Q: Direct address ("you," "your")?**
- Yes, almost every email speaks directly to the reader
- Sometimes — I switch between "you" and "I"
- Rarely — I tend to tell stories in third person

**Q: From your examples, three adjectives keep showing up: [list 4-5 candidates]. Pick the three that capture you best.**
- Multi-select on candidates pulled from observed patterns

**Q: Your top 3 opening moves from your examples look like:**
- Option A (with example line): "..."
- Option B: "..."
- Option C: "..."
- All three / pick differently

## Step 2 — offers

**Q: How many distinct offers do you sell?**
- 1

**Q: For each proof point you give me, I'll ask: do you have a source?**

- No — it's "true enough"

## Step 6 — first draft

**Q: What email do you actually need to send this week?**

- A nurture email (story, framework, observation)
- An announcement

## Format for the wizard

When asking questions:
1. Always present 3-4 specific options based on observed patterns or category norms.
2. Never ask "what's your X?" with no scaffolding.
3. After a multiple-choice, if user picks "Other" or types custom, accept it but ask follow-up if vague.
4. Confirm understanding by reading back what you'll write to the file before writing.
